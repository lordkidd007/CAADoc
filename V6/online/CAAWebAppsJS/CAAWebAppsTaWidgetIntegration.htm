Widget 3D Dashboard Integration
Technical Article

Abstract

The purpose of this article is to present the two ways to integrate a widget into the 3D Dashboard, each one being dedicated to a specific scenario. The Widget App can come from the internet, or can be a 3DEXPERIENCE platform widget [1]

Third Party Apps

"Third Party Apps" is a widget of the 3DEXPERIENCE platform only accessible by its administrators. It enables them to "push" a widget into a platform instance. The widget access can be granted for all people of the platform, or part of them. The granted people have access to the widget through a quadrant of the 3DCompass.

Just above you can see the "CAA My Test" widget integrated into the East Quadrant. The end user drags and drops the widget from the 3D Compass to one of his/her dashboard.

Just below the "Third Party Apps" widget:

On the left, the information about the widget (short and long name, icon, url code, its place in the 3DCompass quadrant), and on the right a preview.

The url must be accessible from the 3D Dashboard. In other words a HTTP/HTTPS request must not fail.

Such widgets are trusted.

Run Your App

"Run Your App" is a widget of the 3DEXPERIENCE platform. It enables any end-user to integrate a Widget App into one of his/her dashboard.

The requested information is the url of the widget application. This url must be accessible from the 3D Dashboard. In other words a HTTP/HTTPS request must not fail.

Once you have clicked on the Add button, the "Run Your App" widget will be instantiated in your current 3D Dashboard.

The widget pointed by the "Run Your App" widget is not trusted, except if the same widget (same url) has also been pushed via "Third Party Apps".

In Short

If your widget is available through a url accessible from the 3D Dashboard you can either use "Run Your App" for personal purposes, or if you are an administrator, use "Third Party Apps" to insert the widget in the 3D Compass.

In the first case, the widget is already instantiated to your current dashboard, while for the second one, the final granted end user will have to drag and drop it from the quadrant to instantiate it where he/she wants to.

A widget instantiated from "Run Your App" is usually not trusted, whereas a widget integrated from "Third Party Apps" is always trusted.
