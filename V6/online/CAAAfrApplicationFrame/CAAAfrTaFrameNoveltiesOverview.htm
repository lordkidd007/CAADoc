IFWE Compass User Experience Novelties
Technical Article

Abstract

The IFWE Compass User Experience provides a unique visual signature as well as a clear and unified access to key services for all 3DEXPERIENCE platform apps. It also applies a brand new graphic charter that is fully compliant with touch devices.

Concerning the CAA User Experience platform, the IFWE Compass User Experience has a big impact on the way to integrate and access interactive commands. This article gives you an overview of these user interface changes.

The IFWE Compass User Experience Signature

The IFWE Compass User Experience provides a new visual signature, redesigning completely global components such as the compass, the access to apps, and Search, Impact, Collaborate, Propagate items of the former bottom bar.

The Compass

The usage and functionality of the Compass has completely changed: the new Compass provides access to apps, formerly known as workbenches, classified by quadrants, replacing the former Start button. The Play button allows to reveal and play available experiences. The Compass is now located on the left of the top bar.

The former PLM Compass, located at the bottom of each model window, use to give you contextual information on a selected object.

The IFWE Compass Versus the Former PLM Compass
    

Note: the information that the former PLM compass used to show can now be accessed from the "Information Intelligence" quadrant of the Compass, the east quadrant, in the B.I. Essentials app.

The Top Bar

The global components, namely the Start button, the Search box, the Impact box, the Collaborate box and the Propagate box used to be located in the bar at the bottom of the application frame. They are now situated in the top bar, at the same level as the new Compass.

How to Access your CAA Apps

If you have defined your own workbenches, you will need to declare them as apps in a service called MyApps[3]. Once this declaration has been done, your apps will be visible in the list of apps accessed  from the Compass. They will appear in the quadrant you have specified in the MyApps server.

When you click on a quadrant of the compass, for example the 3D quadrant, all 3DModeling apps are listed. They are classified by alphabetical order.

Suppression of Menu Bar and Toolbars

With the IFWE Compass User Experience, the menu bar and all the toolbars have been suppressed.

These major changes are the most impacting for your CAA applications. In order to be compliant with the IFWE Compass User Experience, you will need to relocate your commands and toolbars.

The Action Bar

A New Concept

The action bar is a new concept intended to present a more unified user experience across apps (formerly called workbenches), where all toolbars are contained in one single bar displayed at the bottom of the application frame.

The following picture shows a screenshot of the action bar, in the context of Part Design app.

The design of the action bar has been guided by the following principles:

  • Action bar :

    It contains app commands.

  • A clear organization :

    Commands are organized by sections, so that users only see what is relevant to the current context.

  • Progressive disclosure :

    The initial appearance can be expanded or customized to gradually introduce complexity.

  • Multi-platform consistency :

    The action bar supports both touch and mouse/keyboard interactions.

Action Bar Characteristics

The action bar will be the new location of most of the commands in your former CAA toolbars. This section presents the different items of the action bar.

  • A Fixed Area:

    This area is always displayed. It houses standard commands that are frequently needed , such as Copy/Paste, Undo/Redo, etc.

  • Sections:

    Sections represent a new way to group commands in the action bar. One section is visible at the time and can be changed by clicking on another section button. Commands of the action bar necessarily belong to a section.

  • Primary Commands:

    They are the most frequently used commands in a section, and are always visible.

  • Secondary Commands:

    They are commands that aren't frequently used. They are hidden by default and can be accessed by clicking on a an arrow on the right hand side of the action bar.

  • Flyouts:

    They are used to combine very similar commands.

We will go further into the details of the action bar and sections in the following article "IFWE Compass User Experience Adoption Tips"[1].

Tabs Instead of Windows

The CAA application frame is a MDI (Multiple Document Interface) application, able to host several model windows as child windows of the main Frame, with one window active at the time.

With the IFWE Compass User Experience, the windows are now presented as tabs, allowing an easier way to organize and navigate across your content.

In Short

V6R2014 features the new IFWE Compass User Experience based on the 3DEXPERIENCE platform. The impact for your CAA application is mainly the location of your commands in the frame, due to the suppression of the menu bar and all toolbars.

If you have created your own workbenches, you will need to declare them as apps in a service called MyApps[3]. Once your apps are declared, they become visible in the compass and all your commands become accessible. Add-ins remain accessible. By default, your toolbars appear underneath the action bar and a special button in the action bar gives you access to the former menu bar if you had commands stored in menus. It's possible to interactively add your CAA commands in the action bar, via session options customization in the "Me services" menu of the top bar.

It is however important to migrate your commands to adopt the IFWE Compass User Experience principles and make them accessible from the most adequate location in the frame. These migrations can be initiated as from V6R2014. The technical article "IFWE Compass User Experience Adoption Tips" will give you all the necessary recommendations [1] . An example of use case migration is described in the article "Migration of a CAA Geometry sample" [2].
