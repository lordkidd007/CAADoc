What Is a Composites Engineering Design Feature?

The Composites Engineering Design application is a CATIA application in Part Workshop, the Composites Workbench provides a set of tools dedicated to the design of composites parts.

A Composites part is made of plies, cores assembled in a specific order, forming a stacking. Each ply and/or core lies on a surface (in most cases it is the surface of the mold) and has attributes such as: a contour, an associated material, a fiber direction for plies and a solid reference, an associated material for cores.

The aim of the Composites application is to provide several design methodologies to the designer, each one based on a specific set of tools, so that the designer can design all kind of Composites configuration in the most efficient and easiest way.

The provided methodologies take into account specific constraints (constraints related to the manufacturing process that will be used or constraints related to the sub-structure parts assembly context) that have an impact on the final design.

Those methodologies can be classified as followed:

  1. Grid based approach (conceptual design)
  2. Zone based approach (conceptual design)
  3. Ply based approach (detailed design).

The two conceptual design approaches (Zone and Grid) start with a preliminary definition of the composites part, made of zones, cells with constant thickness (laminate). Tools are provided to automatically generate the plies stacking.

Whatever methodology is used, the entities that will be manufactured are the plies and the cores

Composites Design provides dedicated features, that can be instantiated, modified with CAA APIs.

  • Most of the CAA exposed APIs are used to instantiate and modify the plies and/or the cores.
  • Others APIs are used to manage the conceptual definition.

The composites features are based on the General Update Capability infrastructure shared by all features.

General Update Capability

The Composites design application relies on the Feature Modeler infrastructure [1]. Composites features have Specification/Result Modeler behavior. Composites Features are also geometric features that have 3D representation in the 3DShape[2]. They inherit from Mechanical Modeler mechanisms.

Manipulate and use of Composites feature required to know Feature modeler and Mechanical modeler concepts. All CAA topics discussed in Composites APIs largely use references to spec/result mechanisms, 3DShape navigation, geometrical sets, geometrical features, aggregation, update etc.

What Is Inside a Composites Engineering Design Feature?

The Composites Design features illustrate the way the Composites objects are manipulated in real life.

The key physical object used in Composites Design are Plies, Cores (and in less cases Cut Pieces). Those objects correspond to precise physical geometrical descriptions: Plies and Cut Pieces need a contour design on a reference surface, Core need a solid, representing a "reservation" area.

Then Plies, Cores, Cut pieces are structured under the Stacking object's group. Stacking contains Plies Groups, witch contain Sequences of the plies. The Sequences represent the ordered "pass" used to manufacture the Plies, Cut Pieces or Cores in the physical process (commonly called in Composites domain as "the shop floor").

Composites Structural Objects

Stacking , Plies Groups ,Sequence, Plies, Cut Pieces, and Core are structural objects , internally they inherit from the standard "geometrical set" .feature.

  • Plies, Cut Pieces aggregate the Composites Geometry feature.
  • Core points to a solid representation for the reservation aera.

Composites Geometrical Objects

Composites Geometry feature is a generic Geometrical Objects. describing a Contour. Plies, Cut Pieces aggregate a Composites Geometry feature object, and point to associate de Material , Reference surface, Rosette axis, Fiber orientation
