Life Cycle Management

PLM stands for Product Life cycle Management, meaning that products are managed during their whole lifetime as they go through different phases, from development to release. The following figure represents an example of a life cycle:

Fig. 1 - Component life cyle example

Figure 1 denotes all the states of a given PLM component during its lifetime and all the transitions leading from one state to another. Such a diagram is called a Maturity Graph. States are represented as circles, while transitions are symbolized as arrows. These transitions are applied as the result of an external event which name is indicated on the arrow. If a PLM component is in the "In Progress" state, a "Promote" event will trigger the transition that changes its state to "Approved".

Such a maturity graph is used by the PLM Maturity system to manage the PLM components. Every PLM component that is managed will have its state recorded and changed in response to events received.

The Maturity Graph

We have seen that a PLM component life cycle is modeled using a maturity graph. All the elements defined by such a graph are illustrated in the following figure.

Fig. 2 - The elements of a Maturity Graph

 

  • The possible states of a component: "In Progress".
  • Optional behavior associated to a state: each state can specify a list of forbidden operations. "NotRemovable" and "NotEditable" mean that the PLM component will be prevented from being deleted or changed.
  • The events that can be received: "New", "Promote", ...
  • The transitions leading from one state to another, in response to an event.
  • An optional guard on each transaction that indicates whether it can occur or not. The guard ensures that certains conditions are met before allowing the "Promote" transition to take place.

In Short

We have introduced the PLM Maturity: how a graph can be defined so that the Maturity System can manage the PLM components throughout their life-cycle.
